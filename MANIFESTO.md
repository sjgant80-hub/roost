# The roost manifesto

We've watched accommodation become a platform game. Hosts paying 15-17% per booking. Reviews trapped on someone else's database. Guest relationships owned by the algorithm. "Genius" discounts that aren't optional. Delisting threats over policy violations no one can read.

Hosts built the value. Platforms harvest it.

So we're building a different door.

## What roost is

- A **cooperative marketplace** owned by the hosts who list on it
- An **open protocol** anyone can publish to and anyone can build over
- A **zero-commission front-door** that sends guests direct to host sites
- An **anti-platform** by design — if we ever try to extract per-booking rent, the protocol lets hosts walk

## What roost isn't

- Not a VC-backed startup hunting an exit
- Not a "host-friendly" platform that turns hostile once it needs revenue
- Not a private database that hosts have to fight to escape from
- Not the only aggregator — we want others to federate

## The economics

Membership: **£20/month flat** or **£200/year**. That's it.
That covers: Cloudflare hosting, domain, a part-time coordinator, infrastructure.

If we ever have a surplus, **members vote** on what to do with it. Reduce fees. Add features. Host grants. Never executive bonuses. Never investor dividends. Never an acquisition.

## The governance

- 1 host = 1 vote · on all rules, features, money decisions
- A hosts-elected council forms at 50 members and runs operations
- The tech provider (AI Native Solutions) can be replaced by council vote at 100+ members
- All decisions documented publicly · all financials open-book

## The protocol

stays-protocol is the load-bearing layer. It's:
- **CC0 public domain** — no one owns it
- **Geography-agnostic** — UK/Ireland today, global tomorrow
- **Forward-compatible** — won't break as it evolves
- **Federation-ready** — multiple aggregators can co-exist

If roost dies, the protocol lives on. Hosts can switch aggregators by changing nothing — their stays.json on their domain is the source of truth.

## The ask

We need 100 founding hosts.

If you run a sovereign-aligned accommodation business — off-grid, slow-travel, family-run, owner-operated, anything where you'd rather keep your guests than feed them to a platform — apply to join.

[Apply here](host.html#join) · £20/month · cancel anytime · founding-member lifetime fee lock.

If you don't host but you believe the platforms have taken too much: **book direct, every time**. Use roost or anywhere else where the booking actually goes to the host.

## The bet

Three aggregators federating over one open protocol can't be monopoly-rent-extracted. Hosts move freely. Guests have choice. Platforms lose pricing power.

That's the whole bet.
