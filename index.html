Why roost exists · the model
Platforms had 20 years of extracting value from the people who built them.
The next 20: the people own the platform · AI runs it · they oversee it for a couple of hours a month and get on with their lives.

Member-owned co-ops were always the right answer.
They couldn't afford to run before AI made operations cost £0.08/user.
Now they can.

◊ Owned by the hosts

Members co-own roost · 1 host = 1 vote · on every rule, feature, pound. No VCs · no exit · no founder shareholding · structurally unsellable. The first 100 hosts get lifetime founding-member fee-lock at £20/mo.

⚡ Operated by AI

AI agents handle new-host vetting, billing, financial reports, fraud flags, comms. Members prompt + approve · don't have to run the platform themselves. ~2hrs/month of oversight is the whole job.

◬ Zero booking fees · forever

roost takes nothing per booking · ever · in any direction · from guest or host. Bookings go direct host-to-guest. £20/mo flat membership funds infrastructure only.

≋ Open protocol · forkable

stays-protocol is CC0 public domain. Code is MIT. If roost ever drifts, members can fork. That's the discipline. roost is one front door · not the only one possible.

Open protocol

stays-protocol v1 · publish once, syndicate everywhere.

Drop a stays.json file at the root of your site. roost discovers it, indexes it, sends you the guests. Any other aggregator can do the same. No lock-in possible.

// at https://your-domain.com/stays.json { "protocol": "stays-protocol/1.0", "host": { "name": "Wishwood Glamping & Forestry", "location": "Canterbury, Kent", "domain": "https://wishwood.co.uk" }, "stays": [ { "name": "The Hobbit Hut", "sleeps": 3, "price_from": 95, "ical_url": "https://wishwood-engine.workers.dev/ical/hobbit", "book_url": "https://wishwood.co.uk/book/hobbit" } ] }
